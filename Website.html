I'm currently going into my final semester here at the University of Wisconsin-Madison. I'll be graduating in May of 2019 with a Bachelor of Science degree in Computer Science.

 The last (almost) four years at this university have given me some of the most rewarding experiences of my life. UW-Madison has not only provided me with the opportunity to pursue my career in Computer Science but has also given me opportunities to explore new hobbies, form deep connections with peers and leaders of the university, and learn more about my own dreams and goals.

 I've taken a large variety of coursework both in and out of Computer Science. Along with many different CS classes, I had space to take courses in other interests such as Environmental Studies and Sustainability. I've always had a passion for technology, but I've also grown a strong interest in learning more about climate change, renewable energy, sustainable practices, and larger systems thinking. Through taking these classes, I've been able to improve my public speaking skills, become more aware of my own impact of the environment, and think in terms of larger systems rather than short-term impacts.

 Over my time at UW-Madison, I've dabbled in countless different clubs and organizations such as the Software Development club, Game Design & Development, the Tree Climbing club, and the Wisconsin Bike Fed but it wasn't until I joined Hoofers that I really found my calling. Specifically, the Hoofer Outing club and Hoofer Ski & Snowboard club were where I found myself spending the most time and you can find out about what I did with those clubs by clicking here!
